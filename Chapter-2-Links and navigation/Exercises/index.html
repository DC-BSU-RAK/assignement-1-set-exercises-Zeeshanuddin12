The Godfather (1972)

*The Godfather* is an iconic American crime drama of the Corleone mafia family in New York during post-World War II. Don Vito Corleone, the elderly patriarch of the family, is revered and feared. When his life is attempted, his youngest son Michael — who had stayed away from the family's illegal activities — comes forward gradually. When Michael takes over as the head, he becomes a brutal boss, changing the destiny of the family forever.

Key Information

  • Release Date: March 24, 1972
  • Genre: Crime, Drama
  • Runtime: 175 minutes
  • Language: English
  • Country: United States
  • Box Office: Over $250 million worldwide
  • Studio: Paramount Pictures
